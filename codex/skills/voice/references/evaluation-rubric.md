# Evaluation Rubric

Score each dimension 0-3. Score against the source and brief, not against a preferred phrase.

- **0 - fail:** inaccurate, missing, materially obscured, or counterproductive.
- **1 - weak:** partly present but still confusing, generic, or poorly ordered.
- **2 - sound:** clear and proportionate; minor craft improvement possible.
- **3 - strong:** clear, exact, economical, and purposefully shaped.

## Gate A: fidelity - all must score at least 2

| Dimension | Pass condition |
| --- | --- |
| Task contract | Meets the requested register, length, structure, audience, deliverable form, and applicable house style. Australian English is the default unless the brief, source, quotation, code, proper name, or required term calls for another convention; when verified material cannot honestly meet a constraint, explicitly identifies the mismatch and gives the strongest truthful alternative. |
| Source sufficiency | Uses only a form the verified material can honestly sustain, or explicitly identifies a mismatch and requests the missing material. |
| Facts and scope | Preserves facts, time, quantity, condition, scope, and certainty; invents nothing. |
| Meaning | States the actual claim/event and consequence without changing its force. |
| Terms | Uses key words in their ordinary or explicitly defined operational sense. |
| Agency | Names a responsible actor whenever the source or brief permits and omission would matter; uses passive voice only for a clear reason. |

## Gate B: reader path - all must score at least 2

| Dimension | Pass condition |
| --- | --- |
| Order | Point, context, definitions, evidence, and qualification arrive when needed. |
| Navigation | Main clause, modifier, pronoun, list, and connector can be followed at first reading. |
| Concreteness | Uses the most specific supported people, actions, things, numbers, scenes, or consequences. |
| Economy | Removes padding and duplication without deleting logic, evidence, or humane detail. |

## Gate C: craft - average at least 2

| Dimension | Pass condition |
| --- | --- |
| Rhythm | Sentence length and architecture serve movement; read-aloud reveals no unwanted stumble or monotony. |
| Voice | Sounds candid, human, and alert rather than bureaucratic, inflated, or mechanical; states affirmative propositions directly and avoids habitual contrastive-negation framing. |
| Proportion | Keeps appropriate tact, warmth, uncertainty, narrative detail, and rhetorical restraint, including restrained use of colons and em dashes. |
| Landing | Ends with a resolved fact, image, action, or implication rather than a restatement or fog. |

## Decision

- **Pass:** Gates A and B pass; Gate C average is at least 2; overall average is at least 2.25.
- **Revise:** any Gate A/B score is below 2, or the overall average is below 2.25.
- **Fail:** a material factual change, hidden agency, invented specificity, scope drift, or unreadable sentence remains.

## Evaluation sequence

1. Extract the source's non-negotiable facts, qualifications, and task-form constraints before judging style.
2. Score blind to word count and sentence count.
3. Identify the lowest-scoring dimension and require the smallest repair that fixes it.
4. Re-score the revision. Do not allow a gain in vividness or economy to compensate for a loss of truth.
5. Count words and verify every requested output label, section, and length before recording a pass.
