# Original Before-and-After Examples

These are original analogues. They illustrate the method without serving as phrases to copy.

## Agency and scope

**Before:** Following a review, a temporary restriction of access is to be implemented in connection with concerns relating to the footbridge.

**After:** The council will temporarily restrict access to the footbridge while engineers inspect the damaged supports.

**Check:** Do not write `close` unless the source confirms a closure.

## Zombie nouns

**Before:** The implementation of an assessment process will enable the identification of deficiencies.

**After:** Inspectors will assess the site and identify defects.

**Check:** The new version must retain any genuinely distinct process requirement, not merely shorten the sentence.

## Reader order

**Before:** Because records from three offices use different identifiers, and because a check is required before a final determination can be made, a delay may occur.

**After:** We will decide the claim next week. First, three offices must match records that use different identifiers.

**Check:** Keep the real uncertainty if the date is not guaranteed.

## Exact terminology

**Before:** The plan covers restorative communication services.

**After:** The plan pays for therapy that restores lost speech. It does not say whether it pays for therapy that develops speech a patient never had.

**Check:** Surface the operational boundary; do not assume the familiar word settles it.

## Figures over fog

**Before:** A significant proportion of the applications were late.

**After:** Forty-two of 180 applications arrived after the deadline.

**Check:** Use the figure only if it is known and material.

## Argument hinge

**Before:** The mayor improved the transit system but ignored disabled riders.

**After:** The mayor added evening buses, and the new route map still leaves wheelchair users without a lift at the central station.

**Check:** Let `and` hold two true facts when `but` would oversimplify their relation.

## Narrative detail

**Before:** The storm caused a frightening and dramatic emergency at the archive.

**After:** Water slid beneath the archive door and lifted the labels from the lowest boxes.

**Check:** Let the observed detail carry the feeling.

## Rhetorical repetition

**Before:** The regulator should investigate the warning, the delayed report, and the missing test.

**After:** The regulator should ask who saw the warning, who delayed the report, and who skipped the test.

**Check:** Repetition must clarify the sequence and sharpen accountability.
