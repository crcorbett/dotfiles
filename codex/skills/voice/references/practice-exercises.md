# Practice Exercises

Use these cards to test method, not imitation. Work from a supplied fact packet or deliberately flawed original draft. Preserve every fact, qualification, and operational meaning; do not create a model answer by copying a source. Evaluate the result with the rubric.

| ID | Failure class | Defect to diagnose | Required transformation | Pass signals |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| V01 | Delayed point | a long introductory clause postpones the warning or decision | state the warning or decision first; move the cause after it | early main clause; no lost condition |
| V02 | Broken grammar | severed modifier, slippery pronoun, or buried list | reconnect grammar; repeat a name; parallelise the list | one referent per pronoun; scan-friendly items |
| V03 | Hidden agency | passive construction hides the actor or adds padding | activate unless an explicit passive exception applies | agent and action visible; exception stated if retained |
| V04 | Foggy detail | abstract vocabulary, modifier stack, or vague scale | use supported particulars and figures; cut weak modifiers | concrete fact; no invented precision |
| V05 | Muddled movement | poor order, negative logic, prepositional padding, or elegant variation | reveal structure, state the positive claim, simplify, repeat the clear term | real connector; stable reference; varied rhythm |
| V06 | Zombie nouns | nominalisations consume the verbs | convert process-nouns to actors and verbs where safe | fewer abstractions; no loss of complex meaning |
| V07 | Inflated phrasing | padded phrase or duplicated sense | remove padding and accidental repetition | shorter without semantic loss; deliberate repetition explained |
| V08 | Dead language | stock metaphor or generic intensity | replace with a fact, fresh supported image, or deliberate shorthand | image reveals rather than decorates |
| V09 | Near-miss wording | a word is close in sound but wrong in meaning | test degree, implication, and register; choose the exact word | no semantic drift; reason for key choice |
| V10 | Hidden boundary | a crucial term masks an exclusion or conflates concepts | distinguish ordinary and operational meaning; define scope | affected reader knows inclusion, exclusion, and condition |
| V11 | Wrong form | narrative is used where a direct lead is needed, or summary where scene matters | choose direct or narrative form; orient promptly; use a taster only if it earns its place | correct form; chronology and consequence clear |
| V12 | Institutional evasion | report hides responsibility with jargon, passives, acronyms, or poor order | name failure, owner, evidence, corrective action, and remaining uncertainty | accountability visible; facts retained; shorter without flattening |
| V13 | House-style drift | passive evasion, US spelling, contrastive negation, or showy punctuation | use active agency, Australian English, direct affirmative construction, and ordinary sentence punctuation | no lost qualification; negative retained only when meaningful; colon or em dash earns its place |

## Practice protocol

1. Record the exercise ID and method anchor.
2. Extract non-negotiable facts, actor, action, timing, scope, terms, and uncertainty.
3. Draft one transformation without consulting another writer's final version.
4. Score it with the rubric.
5. Record one concrete mismatch between the output and the pass signals, then revise.

For narrative and rhetoric, do not demand one correct sentence. Test orientation, detail selection, causal movement, rhythm, and landing instead.
