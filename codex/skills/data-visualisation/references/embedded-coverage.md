# Embedded method coverage

This standalone skill treats comprehensive coverage as an operational contract. Each module below must remain represented by a method, a concrete rule, and release evidence; a slogan or a style preference is not coverage.

| Coverage module | Operational locations | Release evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Unified visual argument | Foundations, conceptual framework, rules 1 and 22 | The graphic itself states the question, evidence, comparison, and qualification. |
| Retained dimensions and disclosed loss | Foundations, conceptual framework, rules 6 and 15 | Grain, denominator, transformation, coordinate compromise, and material loss are recorded. |
| Overview, detail, and context | Foundations, conceptual framework, rules 9 and 11 | Macro pattern, local inspection, source, and limitation are available without a memory-heavy view switch. |
| Layering and separation | Foundations, conceptual framework, rules 17, 25, and 29 | Primary evidence dominates while guides, containers, and context remain quieter. |
| Repeated frames and small multiples | Foundations, rules 5 and 10 | Mark, scale, interval, and order remain constant across comparable panels. |
| Colour as evidence | Foundations, rules 18 to 21 and 31 | Every hue has a necessary reading, neutral context is explicit, and ordered colour agrees with ordered data. |
| Narrative through time and space | Foundations, rules 15 and 32, examples 2 and 9 | Event/route order and material spatial relation remain recoverable, or lost dimension and alternative are named. |
| Proportional graphical integrity | Foundations, conceptual framework, rules 3 to 7 | Units, baseline/domain, denominator, interval, and visual/data effect are checked. |
| Three independent competences | Foundations, conceptual framework, rule 2 | Domain, statistical, and visual defences are recorded separately. |
| Mark economy and revision | Foundations, rules 14, 23 to 25 | An erasability test covers every non-data layer; a data-ink diagnostic is recorded when measurable. |
| Appropriate reading surface | Foundations, chart selection, rules 8, 12, 13, and 16 | Text, table, table-graphic, chart, and small-multiple alternatives are considered against the reader task. |
| Dense and exact reading | Foundations, chart selection, examples 1, 4, and 7 | Density, exact lookup, distribution, labels, and uncertainty are retained where they affect the conclusion. |
| Scale relationship and discontinuity | Conceptual framework, rule 26, example 10 | Dual scales are challenged; a necessary break is visibly marked with its omitted range and alternative. |
| Editorial production | Editorial production, rules 22, 27 to 30 | Audience, hierarchy, direct explanation, reconciliation, and full/reduced/grayscale/narrow inspection are recorded. |
| Exceptions and independent challenge | Rules 33 and 34, evaluation rubric | The exception names competing evidence and a separate reviewer challenges the rendered output and specification. |

## Maintenance test

When changing this skill, run `python3 scripts/test_embedded_coverage.py` and verify that every coverage module still has all three links: operational method, concrete rule, and release evidence. Update the rulebook, specification gate, rubric, and examples together when a new method changes what a visualisation must prove.
